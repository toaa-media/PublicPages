Designed for Absolute Privacy

Every feature meticulously crafted to protect your digital life.

Military-Grade Encryption

Files are encrypted using AES-256 before they even touch your storage. It is mathematically impossible to decode without your master password.

Stealth Mode

Disguise SharpVault as an innocent Calculator app. It disappears from your recent apps list and blocks screenshots globally. Open it via a secret dialer code.

Decoy Vault

Forced to open your phone? Enter a fake PIN to show an empty, decoy vault to protect your real data.

Secure Notes

Write private journals, store passwords, and attach photos in a locally encrypted text editor.

Intruder Capture

Someone trying to guess your PIN? SharpVault silently snaps a photo of the intruder using the front camera and saves it to your security log with timestamp and location.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about SharpVault's security architecture and premium features.

What encryption does SharpVault use? +
SharpVault utilizes military-grade AES-256-GCM encryption. Your data is encrypted locally on your device, and only your master password can decrypt it. We leverage the Android Keystore system with hardware-backed security (where available) to ensure your encryption keys never leave the secure enclave of your processor.
Is my data stored on SharpVault servers? +
No. SharpVault is built on a zero-knowledge philosophy. We do not have servers that store your private files, passwords, or encryption keys. Everything stays localized on your device. For extra safety, you can enable Encrypted Cloud Backup to your personal Google Drive, which only YOU can access.
What is Stealth Mode and how does it work? +
Stealth Mode is our signature privacy feature. You can choose to disguise SharpVault as a fully functional Calculator or hide the app icon entirely. When hidden, the app can only be launched by entering a secret code in your phone's dialer or a specific key combination, making it invisible to anyone peaking at your phone.
What happens if someone tries to force me to open the vault? +
We've implemented a Fake Vault (Decoy) feature for exactly this scenario. You can set up a secondary password that opens a completely different, "safe" vault with harmless content. To an intruder, it looks like the real thing, while your sensitive data remains hidden in the primary vault.
How does Intruder Detection work? +
If someone attempts to unlock your vault with the wrong password, SharpVault silently snaps a photo using the front camera and logs the attempt. When you next log in successfully, you'll receive a notification with the intruder's photo, the time of the attempt, and what they tried to access.
Does SharpVault support fingerprint or face unlock? +
Yes. For seamless and secure access, SharpVault integrates with your device\'s biometric sensors. You can enable Fingerprint, Face Unlock, or Iris authentication within the settings to open your vault instantly without typing your PIN every time.
What happens if I lose my phone? +
Since SharpVault is a zero-knowledge app, your data is primarily stored on your device. However, if you have enabled Encrypted Cloud Backup, you can easily restore your entire vault onto a new Android device by logging into your personal Google Drive and providing your master encryption key.
Can I hide the app icon from my home screen? +
Absolutely. Our Stealth Mode allows you to hide the app icon entirely. To access the app when hidden, you simply dial a secret code (e.g., #1234) in your phone\'s dialer, or use our functional Calculator Disguise where the vault only opens after entering your secret PIN.
Can SharpVault store other file types like PDFs or notes? +
Yes. Beyond photos and videos, SharpVault includes a Secure Notes feature for sensitive text data and a Private File Manager that supports documents (PDF, DOCX), audio files, and encrypted archives. All files are protected by the same military-grade encryption.
Is my private data backed up automatically? +
Privacy is our priority, so we don\'t backup your data to our servers. However, you can enable Auto-Sync to your personal Google Drive. The data is encrypted before it leaves your device, so even Google cannot see the contents of your backup.
